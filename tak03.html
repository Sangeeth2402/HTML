Place to visit in Erode :


Birds Sanctuary, Vellode

Birds Sanctuary, Vellode is about 15 kms from Erode. It is at a large lake surrounded with semi-dark bushes near Vellode. This .772 km2 (0.298 sq mi) sanctuary is home to many foreign birds. The sanctuary features thousands of birds coming from various countries, some of which can be easily identified. Some easily found bird species include cormorants, teals, pintail ducks, pelicans, and darters.

Kalingarayan Canal

Kalingarayan Canal is a 56.2-mile (90.5 km) long irrigation canal in the Erode district, Tamil Nadu, India. It was constructed by Kongu chieftain Kalingarayan. The canal’s source is the Kalingarayan Anicut near Bhavani. The canal irrigates approximately 15,743 acres (6,371 ha) of agricultural land. The project including construction of the dam and canal started in 1271 and was completed in 1283.


Exploring Erode District: A Textile Haven

Erode District, often referred to as the “Textile Hub of South India,” is renowned worldwide for its vibrant textile industry. Home to a multitude of weaving clusters, textile mills, and bustling markets, the district is a treasure trove for textile enthusiasts and traders alike. From traditional handloom sarees to modern fabric innovations, Erode’s textile landscape offers a diverse array of products catering to every taste and preference. Embark on a journey through its winding streets and vibrant bazaars to witness the craftsmanship and creativity that define this thriving textile hub.


Historical Landmarks

Step back in time and explore the rich historical heritage of Erode District through its architectural marvels and ancient monuments. Visit the fascinating Thindal Murugan Temple Erode, an ancient shrine dedicated to Lord Murugan, adorned with intricate carvings and steeped in mythological significance. Discover the remnants of the majestic Erode Fort, built by the Vijayanagara Empire, which stands as a testament to the region’s tumultuous past and architectural grandeur.

Temples in Erode:

Sangameswarar temple

Sangameswarar temple (also called Thirunana and Thirukooduthurai) is a temple in Bhavani, in the Erode district, of the Indian state of Tamil Nadu. It is a Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is 15 km from Erode, 30 km from Gobichettipalayam, 56 km from Salem and 106 km from Coimbatore. The temple was built at the confluence of rivers Kaveri, Bhavani and the mystic Amutha (Agaya Gangai).The temple was praised in old Tamil literature as Thirunana. The place is also known as Dakshina Triveni Sangamam and Kooduthurai indicating the confluence of three rivers. Devotees perform last rites for ancestors on the riverbank and offer prayers at the Sangameshwarar temple

Thindal Murugan Temple:

Nestled amidst serene surroundings, Thindal Murugan Temple stands as a testament to the region’s deep-rooted religious heritage and architectural splendor. Dedicated to Lord Murugan, the temple is believed to date back several centuries, with legends shrouding its origins in myth and folklore. Adorned with intricate carvings and vibrant sculptures depicting scenes from Hindu mythology, the temple exudes a sense of divine tranquility and spiritual fervor. Pilgrims and devotees flock to its hallowed precincts to seek blessings, perform rituals, and partake in the age-old traditions that have been passed down through generations.

Bhavani Sagar Dam

Also known as the Lower Bhavani Dam, it is one of the largest earthen dams in Asia. Constructed across the Bhavani River, the dam is a crucial water resource for agriculture, providing irrigation water to the surrounding districts. The reservoir created by the dam, known as the Lower Bhavani Reservoir, also supports drinking water supply and hydroelectric power generation.

Kodiveri Dam

Situated on the Bhavani River near Gobichettipalayam in Erode District, the Kodiveri Dam is a multipurpose project primarily aimed at irrigation and water supply. The scenic surroundings of the dam attract tourists and locals alike, offering opportunities for picnics, boating, and leisurely walks along the reservoir.

Varatupallam dam

Varatu Pallam Dam is an artificial dam built on a hill 12 km (7.5 mi) from Andhiur in Erode district. This dam provides irrigation to the nearby areas. A mountain pass runs from Andhiur to Kollegal. The storage capacity of this reservoir is 1.39 TMC. It is one of the places in Erode district which receives 1200 mm rainfall. Canals and tanks with a water storage area of about 5,500 acres Varatu Pallam Dam is located adjacent to the forest area. Due to this, the movement of wild animals like deer, bison, monkey and birds is high. Elephants flock to the dam to drink water in the afternoon and evening. Extra care is needed at times like these. Wild animals like elephant, deer, leopard, wildebeest, porcupine, leopard live in the Varatu Pallam dam forest near Andhiur in Erode district. Elephants and deers come to Varatu Pallam dam to drink water at 3 times morning, afternoon and evening. It will be a sight to behold.

Kunderipallam Dam

Kunderipallam Dam is a dam located in Kongarpalayam, Gobichettipalayam Taluk, Erode district, Tamil Nadu, India.Located in the southern part of a dense hill forest, it is a crucial structure for Tamil Nadu’s agricultural and hydroelectric power sectors. Its value as a tourist spot and habitat for various species of fish and birds further raises its significance. The dam is built across wild streams at the confluence of Kadambur Hill and an adjacent hill.It is a small dam, supporting agriculture on roughly 3000 acres (1214 hectares), mostly by increasing the ground water level rather than direct canal irrigation. Steep hills with dense jungle take off on both the right and left (East and West) sides of the dam, where wild varieties of flora and fauna are seen.

Burgur Hills

The Tamil Nadu government has declared 80,114.80 hectares of reserve forests in Bargur Hills in Erode district, as the Thanthai Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary. This stretch of protected forests, links the Nilgiris Biosphere Reserve with Cauvery South Wildlife Sanctuary, and has been notified as the 18th wildlife sanctuary in the State. (Ms) No. 19, dated January 30, 2024, said these forests occupies a prominent position in the Eastern Ghats as they merge with the Western Ghat at the Nilgiris. The vast landscape is home to diverse flora and fauna, making it an ideal habitat for various life forms. The landscape is interconnected to the Kollegal forests of Karnataka and the Nilgiris, creating one of the most diverse habitats in the region.
